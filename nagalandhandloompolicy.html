Policies of Nagaland


The government of Nagaland has implemented various policies to promote and develop the handloom sector in the state. These policies aim to provide financial assistance, training, and marketing support to weavers and artisans. Some of the notable handloom policies are:




1. Nagaland Handloom and Handicrafts Development Policy: The policy aims to promote and develop the handloom and handicrafts sector in Nagaland by providing financial assistance, training, and marketing support to weavers and artisans. The policy also aims to create employment opportunities in the handloom and handicrafts sector.

2. Cluster Development Programme for Handlooms: The programme aims to develop handloom clusters in Nagaland to promote the handloom sector. The programme provides financial assistance for the development of infrastructure, technology upgradation, and marketing support to weavers and artisans. The programme also provides training to weavers and artisans.

3. State Handloom Development Fund: The fund aims to provide financial assistance to handloom weavers in Nagaland. The fund provides loans to weavers for the purchase of raw materials, equipment, and other necessary items for weaving. The fund also provides subsidies for the purchase of raw materials.

4. Nagaland Handloom and Handicrafts Development Corporation (NSHHDC): The corporation aims to provide support to weavers and artisans in Nagaland by promoting the formation of weavers' and artisans' cooperatives. The corporation provides financial assistance for the establishment of cooperatives and provides training and marketing support to weavers and artisans.

5. National Handloom Development Programme (NHDP): The policy aims to promote and develop the handloom sector in Nagaland by providing financial assistance for the purchase of raw materials, equipment, and other necessary items for weaving. The policy also provides training to weavers and artisans.

These policies have helped in promoting and developing the handloom sector in Nagaland and have provided support to weavers and artisans.
