Schemes of Mizoram


The government of Mizoram has implemented several schemes to promote horticulture in the state. Some of the key horticulture schemes of the government of Mizoram are:




1. New Land Use Policy (NLUP): The New Land Use Policy is a flagship scheme of the government of Mizoram, which aims to promote sustainable agriculture and horticulture practices in the state. Under this scheme, farmers are provided financial assistance for the development of horticulture activities on their land. The scheme focuses on promoting the cultivation of high-value crops, such as fruits and vegetables.

2. Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North East Region (MOVCD-NER): The Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North East Region is a centrally-sponsored scheme implemented by the government of Mizoram. The scheme aims to promote organic farming and horticulture practices in the state. Under this scheme, farmers are provided financial assistance for the development of organic horticulture activities on their land.

3. Integrated Horticulture Development Scheme (IHDS): The Integrated Horticulture Development Scheme is a centrally-sponsored scheme implemented by the government of Mizoram. The scheme aims to promote the development of horticulture activities in the state. Under this scheme, farmers are provided financial assistance for the development of horticulture activities on their land. The scheme focuses on promoting the cultivation of high-value crops, such as fruits and vegetables.

4. National Horticulture Mission (NHM): The National Horticulture Mission is a centrally-sponsored scheme implemented by the government of Mizoram. The scheme aims to promote the development of horticulture activities in the state. Under this scheme, farmers are provided financial assistance for the development of horticulture activities on their land. The scheme focuses on promoting the cultivation of high-value crops, such as fruits and vegetables.

These schemes aim to promote the growth of the horticulture sector in Mizoram, create employment opportunities for farmers, and improve the income of farmers. The schemes also focus on improving productivity, promoting sustainable farming practices, and ensuring food security in the state.
